## Design Context

### Users
Craft beer enthusiasts browsing and ordering from a physical craft beer shop in Voronezh, Russia ("ПРАЙМ БИР"). Primarily mobile — users scroll through styles on their phone while deciding what to buy, or while physically in the store. Russian-speaking audience. The product is tactile, sensory, about discovery.

### Brand Personality
- **Warm, tactile, wet** — beer is a physical product. Foam, liquid, glass, ice, condensation.
- **Crafted, artisanal** — not mass-market. Small brewery vibes, labels with hand-painted character.
- **Playful, not corporate** — the UI has beer-pouring animations, foam bubbles, liquid glass. It leans into its theme rather than being a generic catalog.
- Three words: **artisanal, liquid, tactile**.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Refined-craft — the design is polished but playful. Think: a museum exhibit caption for a beer, not an enterprise dashboard.
- **Theme**: Light (foam-cream background with style-colored accents). The foam header/gradient represents the "head" of a pour; the body color represents the beer itself.
- **Reference**: A physical shop where each style has a color identity. The interface should feel like handling real bottles.
- **Anti-references**: Dashboard admin UI, SaaS templates, flat marketing sites, generic e-commerce.
- **Color**: Neutral cream (`#FFF8E7` → `#EDD9AB`) with dynamic accent per beer style (amber, orange, pink, brown, blue, etc.).

### Design Principles
1. **Motion simulates physics, not UI tropes.** Pours fall, liquid rises, foam rises. Animations should feel like matter, not bouncy cards.
2. **The beer style is the hero.** Color, typography, and mood shift with the selected style. The app is a frame for the beer.
3. **Liquid glass is used purposefully.** Transparent surfaces reveal the bubbles and foam behind them — this IS the product, not decoration.
4. **Russian-first typography.** All display faces must support Cyrillic properly. Don't pick a font that breaks on Russian text.
5. **Tactile, not flat.** Subtle texture, warm neutrals, inset shadows — but never skeuomorphic. It's the modern version of a beer label.
